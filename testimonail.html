Fertilizers

DAP (Diammonium Phosphate)
Composition: 18% Nitrogen, 46% Phosphorus.
Uses: Excellent source of phosphorus and nitrogen; suitable for early plant growth.
Properties: Granular, dissolves quickly, and lowers soil pH initially.
Caution: Overuse may lead to soil acidity.

MOP (Muriate of Potash) Composition: 60% Potassium.
Uses: Promotes flowering, fruiting, and overall plant resilience; used for potassium-deficient soils.
Properties: Highly soluble in water; widely used in all types of soils.
Caution: High chloride content may harm chloride-sensitive crops.

TSP (Triple Superphosphate)
Composition: 46% Phosphorus.
Uses: Best for phosphorus-deficient soils; promotes root and seedling development.
Properties: Granular form, less water-soluble compared to DAP and MAP.
Caution: Should be used in soils with neutral to alkaline pH for maximum efficiency.

SOP (Sulphate of Potash)
Composition: 50% Potassium, 18% Sulfur.
Uses: Ideal for chloride-sensitive crops like tobacco, potatoes, and citrus.
Properties: Provides sulfur, which is essential for protein synthesis; does not contain chloride. Caution: More expensive than MOP.

MAP (Monoammonium Phosphate) Composition: 11% Nitrogen, 52% Phosphorus.
Uses: Preferred for crops requiring high phosphorus during early growth stages.
Properties: Acidic in nature, making it effective in neutral to alkaline soils.
Caution: Should not be mixed with alkaline fertilizers.

Urea (46% Nitrogen)
Composition: High nitrogen content (46%).
Uses: Commonly used for all crops to boost vegetative growth.
Properties: Highly soluble in water; suitable for both soil application and foliar sprays.
Caution: Prone to volatilization, so it should be incorporated into the soil after application.
